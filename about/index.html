About Me

Most recently I have spent the past 7 months backpacking through Latin America. It was a wonderful trip one of the highlights of which was teaching in a school in Bogotá (see Communication for more).

I am currently looking for my next position, a job that I can throw myself into and make use of problem solving, analytical thinking and communication skills.

Background

Last April I completed a 2 year Post-doc in Leiden University, working with Anthony Brown on the development of the Gaia archive while continuing my research in star formation.

Before that I successfully defended my thesis in October 2013 on Accretion Variability in Young Stellar Objects. (Graduated December 2014). During my thesis I spent some time in a number of institutes. I began my PhD in Ireland, under a joint scholarship between the Dublin Institute of Advanced Studies (DIAS) and Armagh Observatory, working with Tom Ray, Aleks Scholz and Jorick Vink. After being awarded the ESO studentship, I continued my studies at the ESO headquarters, Garching, Germany.
